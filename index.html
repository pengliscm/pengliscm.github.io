Biography

Dr. Peng Li is a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Marketing, Management, and Supply Chain at the Woody Hunt College of Business, UTEP. He earned his first Ph.D. in Civil & Environmental Engineering from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee and is completing his second Ph.D. in Supply Chain Management from Rutgers Business School. Dr. Li has published more than 20 peer-reviewed papers across supply chain management, logistics, transportation engineering, and operations management.

His research lies at the intersection of engineering and business, focusing on supply chain management, stochastic optimization, queueing theory, and machine learning. Dr. Li's work demonstrates a commitment to addressing real-world challenges, including sustainability in logistics and transportation systems. One of his recent projects involves developing a decentralized cross-sector supply chain model to improve policy-making for scarce resource allocation.

At UTEP, Dr. Li teaches courses in Production Planning & Control, Logistics Management, and Transportation & Warehousing Systems. He emphasizes a student-centered teaching approach that combines theoretical knowledge with practical applications. Dr. Li is particularly excited about introducing industry-partnered projects into his classes, allowing students to work on real-world supply chain challenges, such as his research on Electric Vehicle Battery Swapping (EVBS) operations.

Beyond academics, Dr. Li enjoys swimming, reading, listening to music, and camping with his family and friends. He finds inspiration in Frank Wilczek's book, A Beautiful Question: Finding Nature's Deep Design, which emphasizes the aesthetic principles underlying scientific inquiry. This philosophy shapes his teaching approach, encouraging students to appreciate the interconnectedness and beauty of knowledge.
